About Me

I am Yuexin Liao, a senior student at Caltech. I am passionate about creating new theories at the intersection of mathematics, computer science, and economics. My research concentrates on partial differential equations, dynamical systems, machine learning, and decision theory.

At the core of my work is a rigorous exploration and formulation of distribution lawsâ€”whether manifesting as intricate statistical patterns in data science, or analytical structures and abstract probabilistic frameworks within pure mathematics.

I perceive programming as a formal linguistic framework, language itself as a hierarchy of structural abstractions, and mathematics as the foundational syntax describing universal regularities. This integrated and generative perspective fuels my enthusiasm for theoretical elegance, structural unification, and deep explanatory power.

My current research interests include:

(1) Mathematics for AI. The expressive power that probability theory and statistical physics provide lies in transforming deep neural networks from complex algorithmic objects into analyzable physical systems. This perspective allows us to interpret phenomena such as overparameterization, generalization, and convergence through the lenses of phase transitions and criticality, thereby establishing rigorous theoretical guarantees. Another line of work aims to mathematize domain axioms and embed them into neural network architectures by solving corresponding optimization problems, developing finely controlled networks that respect these axioms.

(2) Decision theory and Data Science. This part of my research seeks to endow decision with rigorous underlying mathematical structure through formal axiomatizations and measures.

Network Flow Probabilistic Approach to Analyze Decision Phenomena
Draft, polishing for arXiv (available upon request) This work uses network structure to create a new measure of irrationality which has provably superior sensitivity to choice perturbations compared with existing measures, according to probabilistic analysis. Draft, polishing for arXiv (available upon request) This work develops a powerful combinatorial dissection method that solves an intractable geometric problem and provides a combinatorial parametrization as well as the exact volume of the weak order flow polytope in certain dimensions.
