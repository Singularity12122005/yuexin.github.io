About

I am Yuexin Liao, a senior student at Caltech, where I am extremely fortunate to receive guidance and mentorship from Prof. Steven Low. I am passionate about creating new theories at the intersection of mathematics, computer science, and economics. My research concentrates on optimization, stochastic analysis, machine learning and algorithm design.

Grounded in pure mathematics, particularly in analysis, probability and combinatorics, my expertise lies in the design of provably efficient algorithms. I am driven by the ambition to construct abstract, theoretically principled frameworks that power impactful solutions across complex real-world domains, including energy systems, computational finance, and economics.

Furthermore, I perceive programming as a formal linguistic framework, language itself as a hierarchy of abstractions, and mathematics as the foundational syntax describing universal regularities. This integrated and generative perspective fuels my enthusiasm for theoretical elegance, deep explanatory power and real-world impact. I always welcome new opportunities for collaboration and discussions on interesting projects. Feel free to contact me if you would like to chat!

Work in Progress

  • Analytical Solutions Of Unbalanced Three-phase Symmetric Radial Networks with Transformers. Coming soon.
  • Network Flow Probabilistic Approach to Analyze Behavior Rationality. Draft, polishing for arXiv. This work uses network structure to create a new measure of irrationality which has provably superior sensitivity to choice perturbations compared with existing measures, according to probabilistic analysis. We develop an algorithm to compute the measure with better computational complexity.
  • Analytic Dissection of the Weak Order Flow Polytope: A Combinatorial Parametrization and Exact Volume Formula for some n. Draft, polishing for arXiv. This work develops a powerful combinatorial dissection method that solves an intractable geometric problem and provides a combinatorial parametrization as well as the exact volume of the weak order flow polytope in certain dimensions.
